The Case of Kaya

On October 07, Kaya—an affectionate companion and member of her household—was wrongfully subjected to an electric shock by a remote collar for stepping off her designated pad. There was no imminent risk, no prior notice, and no proportional justification. The action constituted a punitive measure incompatible with humane standards.

Our Position

  • Disproportionate Punishment: The response far exceeded any alleged infraction.
  • Lack of Due Process: No clear warning, training alternative, or fair opportunity to comply.
  • Humane Standards: Electronic shock devices raise serious welfare concerns and should not be normalized.

Our firm has taken this matter pro bono. We will pursue remedies proportionate to the harm, advocate for humane practices, and elevate standards that protect companions from unnecessary force.
